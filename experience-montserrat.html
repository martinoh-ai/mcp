A Spiritual Journey

The Magic Mountain

Rising dramatically from the Catalan plain, Montserrat's serrated peaks have inspired awe and devotion for centuries. The name means "serrated mountain" in Catalan, and its unique rock formations‚Äîsculpted by millions of years of wind and water‚Äîcreate one of Spain's most mystical landscapes.

At its heart sits the Santa Maria de Montserrat, a Benedictine abbey founded in 1025. Here, pilgrims come to venerate La Moreneta (the Black Madonna), one of Europe's most beloved religious icons. But Montserrat is more than a pilgrimage site‚Äîit's a place where spirituality, nature, art, and Catalan identity intertwine.

Whether you come for the monastery, the mountain hikes, the famous boys' choir, or the world-class museum, Montserrat offers an experience unlike any other.

Montserrat Highlights

The Black Madonna

Visit La Moreneta in the basilica‚Äîa 12th-century wooden statue said to have healing powers. Touch the orb in her hand as millions have done before you.

Escolania Boys' Choir

One of Europe's oldest boys' choirs, documented since 1223. Hear them sing at 1pm (Mon-Fri) or noon on Sundays‚Äîa sublime experience.

Mountain Trails

Take the funicular to Sant Joan and hike to abandoned hermitages. The views from Sant Jeroni peak (1,236m) stretch to the Pyrenees and the sea.
